Ugly Websites

It was interesting to find out that the reason that 'ugly' websites sell better was that while a website might be
pretty or cool it doesn't always work and can be slow to load because of all of the elements, especially if it has
so kind of animation. The best kind of website is one that is simple with fewer elements and little to no animation.

The article also said that there's an uptick in images with types that don't match what the website is about or trying
to sell. If the website isn't selling something or if it's meant to be more of an artist page or blog it would be fine, but
if the sole purpose of the website is to sell something and it putting all of its effort into how the website looks without taking
into consideration how the website works, than that's were it can become a problem.

It also said that when the design of a website is taking how a website looks over the functionality of the
website, it becomes a problem when new designers and clients think that all websites should look like that.

I liked how the article showed why some of the designs wouldn't work, they didn't just say why
the design wouldn't work, they also showed how to fix it.
